What is VSEPR?

VSEPR predicts 3D molecular geometry from the repulsion between electron pairs (bonding + lone) in the valence shell of the central atom.

Repulsion Order

Lone–Lone > Lone–Bond > Bond–Bond
Each lone pair compresses bond angles by roughly 2–2.5°.

Hybridisation Link

2 pairs → sp (linear) · 3 pairs → sp² (planar) · 4 pairs → sp³ (tetrahedral) · 5 pairs → sp³d · 6 pairs → sp³d²

Polarity Rule

A molecule is polar if bond dipoles do not cancel. Symmetrical molecules (CO₂, CH₄, CCl₄) are non-polar even with polar bonds.

Coordinate Bonds

In dative/coordinate bonds a lone pair is donated to an empty orbital (e.g. NH₃ → H⁺ gives NH₄⁺). Shape is still predicted by VSEPR.

Limitations

Fails for transition-metal complexes (use CFT/LFT), molecules with relativistic effects, and cannot predict exact angles for polyatomic ligands.
